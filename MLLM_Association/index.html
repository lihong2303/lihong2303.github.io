The Labyrinth of Links: Navigating the Associative Maze of Multi-modal LLMs

Hong Li, Nanxi Li, Yuanjie Chen, Jianbin Zhu, Yong-Lu Li
Shanghai Jiao Tong University
Paper Video Demo Code Data

Abstract

Multi-modal Large Language Models (MLLMs) have exhibited powerful capability across various tasks. However, recently more and more deficiencies of MLLMs have been found compared to human intelligence, e.g., hallucination. To pave the way toward human intelligence, the community dedicated efforts to building larger benchmarks with more complex tasks. Here, we aim to benchmark an essential but usually overlooked ability of intelligence, i.e., association. To comprehensively investigate MLLMs's performance on association, we devise a standard association benchmark based on adjective and verb association semantic concepts. Instead of costly data annotation and organization, we propose a convenient annotation-free reconstruction method transforming the general dataset for our association tasks. Furthermore, we employ two strategies to comprehensively investigate the MLLMs' ability and potential, and an adapted tuning-free prompt engineering method, a complex Mix-of-Expert (MOE) model to investigate the MLLM's zero-shot capability. Our systematic investigation shows that current open-source MLLMs consistently exhibit poor capability in association tasks. Even the currently state-of-the-art MLLM and GPT-4V(vision) also have a significant gap compared to humans.

What is Association?

In the process of human thinking, there is a psychological process of automatically or consciously connecting to other relevant concepts, situations, or memories through a certain concept, personal experience, or external stimulus.
